About Me

I’m Marcus, a Junior Software Developer with a background in designing accessible digital learning experiences and a strong foundation in C#, .NET, SQL and collaborative engineering practices. For seven years, I worked in Learning and Development, creating digital learning content and improving the way people interact with online tools. Over time, I became increasingly aware of how often digital products overlook accessibility and reliability, and that realisation is what sparked my interest in building better technology and exploring a career switch.

Retraining as a developer gave me the chance to explore that interest more deeply. During the Northcoders Enterprise Engineering bootcamp, I learned how to build reliable, testable systems and how to work effectively within a collaborative engineering team. Since graduating, I have continued expanding my skills through personal projects, including Calico, where I led accessibility implementation, and Hobby Hub, where I taught myself React and TypeScript to build a full stack hobby discovery platform.

I enjoy working in environments where curiosity, teamwork and continuous improvement are encouraged. I am especially motivated by roles where accessibility, usability and thoughtful engineering come together to create products that work for everyone. I care about clarity, problem solving and building technology that supports people in a simple and inclusive way.

I am currently looking for opportunities where I can grow as a developer, contribute to accessible product development and keep learning from experienced engineers. This website is a place to share my projects, document what I am learning and reflect on the journey that brought me into software development.
